Political History

Arunachal Pradesh transitioned from a non-party political scenario to a multi-party system in the 1970s. The Indian National Congress (INC) was the first to establish a presence, followed by other national and regional parties.

Current Political Scenario

Today, both states have a vibrant political ecosystem with major parties like INC and BJP competing alongside regional parties such as PPA and AC. The political discourse revolves around development, infrastructure, and social welfare.

Ideology and Objectives

  • Indian National Congress (INC): Center-left, secular, liberal; focuses on social welfare, development, and national integration.
  • Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP): Right-wing, nationalist, conservative; emphasizes development, good governance, and cultural nationalism.
  • People's Party of Arunachal (PPA): Regional, secular, democratic; prioritizes state-specific issues, tribal welfare, and regional development.
  • Arunachal Congress (AC): Regional, liberal, democratic; focuses on state-specific issues, youth empowerment, and social justice.

Analysis

Regional parties like PPA and AC play a vital role in Arunachal Pradesh, as they cater to the unique needs and concerns of the state's diverse population. While national parties have a stronger presence, regional parties often emerge as kingmakers in coalition governments, influencing state politics and policy decisions. Their importance lies in their ability to address local issues, promote regional development, and provide an alternative to national parties.

Nature of Party Politics

Party politics in Arunachal Pradesh is characterized by the actions and behaviors of party workers and functionaries for the welfare of their respective parties. It is not driven by self-interest or selfish motives. The state has witnessed the growth of multiple political parties, each with its own ideology and objectives.
