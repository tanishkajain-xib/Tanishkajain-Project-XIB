Explore the Vibrant Village of Arunachal Pradesh

Village: Duling

Location: Bordering China, in the Upper Siang district of Arunachal Pradesh

Population: Approximately 500 inhabitants

Accessibility: Reachable by road from Yingkiong, the district headquarters (150 km)

Historical Significance: Duling is the easternmost village of India, sharing a border with China. It played a crucial role in the 1962 Indo-China war.

Uniqueness: Duling is known for its stunning natural beauty, with the Siang River flowing through it. The village is home to the Adi community, with their unique culture and traditions.

Nearest Tourist Attractions:

  • Yingkiong (district headquarters)
  • Tuting (known for its natural beauty and trekking trails)
  • Gelling (a scenic village with ancient ruins)

Transformation

From "India's last village" to "India's first village," Duling's transformation symbolizes the government's focus on developing border villages. This initiative will boost tourism, create jobs, and enhance the quality of life for the villagers.

Must-Visit Destination

Duling offers an unparalleled cultural and natural experience. Witness the breathtaking scenery, learn about the Adi community's traditions, and explore the historic significance of this border village.
