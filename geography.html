Unknown Facts

  • Sikkim is home to the world's third-highest peak, Kanchenjunga. This towering mountain is not only a geographical marvel but also a spiritual symbol for the people of Sikkim.
  • Arunachal Pradesh has over 500 species of orchids, many found nowhere else in the world. The state's rich biodiversity includes these unique orchids, adding to its allure for nature enthusiasts and researchers alike.
  • Sikkim is the first fully organic state in India, with a complete ban on chemical fertilizers. This landmark achievement underscores Sikkim's commitment to sustainable agriculture and environmental stewardship.

Sikkim

  • Tourism:
    • Sikkim is known for its breathtaking landscapes including Kanchenjunga mountain, lush valleys, and serene monasteries.
    • It offers adventure tourism activities like trekking, mountaineering, and river rafting.
    • The state's rich biodiversity attracts nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers.
  • Floriculture:
    • Sikkim is a major producer of flowers like orchids, gladioli, roses, and cymbidium.
    • The state's floriculture industry has seen significant growth and contributes to its economy.
  • Agriculture:
    • Sikkim is India's first organic state, emphasizing sustainable and eco-friendly agricultural practices.
    • The state produces a variety of crops including cardamom, ginger, oranges, and tea.
  • Hydroelectric Power:
    • Sikkim has immense potential for hydroelectric power generation due to its numerous rivers and steep terrain.
    • The state has been harnessing this potential and has become a significant contributor to India's hydroelectric power production.
  • Weaving:
    • Sikkim has a rich tradition of weaving, with intricate designs and vibrant colors reflecting the cultural heritage of the state.
    • Handloom products like carpets, blankets, and traditional attire are popular among locals and tourists.

Arunachal Pradesh

  • Tourism:
    • Arunachal Pradesh's diverse topography offers opportunities for adventure tourism including trekking, river rafting, and angling.
    • The state is home to a rich cultural heritage, with numerous tribal festivals and traditional crafts attracting tourists.
  • Agriculture:
    • Arunachal Pradesh's agriculture is mainly subsistence-based, with rice being the primary crop along with maize, millet, and wheat.
    • The state's agro-climatic conditions are suitable for horticulture and floriculture.
  • Hydroelectric Power:
    • With abundant water resources from its rivers, Arunachal Pradesh has immense potential for hydroelectric power generation.
    • The state has several ongoing and proposed hydroelectric projects to harness this potential.
  • Handicrafts:
    • Arunachal Pradesh is known for its exquisite wood carvings, cane and bamboo crafts, and traditional handwoven textiles.
    • These handicrafts are an integral part of the state's cultural identity and contribute to its economy.
  • Minerals:
    • Arunachal Pradesh is rich in mineral resources such as coal, dolomite, limestone, graphite, and quartzite.
    • However, sustainable and responsible mining practices are essential to preserve the state's pristine environment.
